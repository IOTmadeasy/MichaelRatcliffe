Motivation

570 million farms in the world. More than 90% of farms are run by an individual or a family and rely primarily on family labour.

Where and why

Six Continents: Farmers, Aggtech, Academics, GO/NGO.

Technology adoption

In oder for technology to be adopted by farmers it must be:

-Useful
-Avaliable
-Affordable
-Known about

Innovators and Laggards

Innovators and Early Adopters take the risks and reap the most reward
Early majority are risk averse but know a low risk opportunity
The rest are left to play catch up
Where you are on the curve depends on your networking and wider industry participation

Is it useful

Cool does not mean useful

Data does not mean valuable

Technology should be used to make your life easier as a farmer

Computational Vision

Cheap hardware smart software = scalable and affordable

The tractor changed the way we view manual labor on the farm, the tractor can outlift and outpull even the strongest of farm labours and increased farming productivity.

AI and computational vision is set to do the same with mental demands of farming, likley outperforming even the smartest of specialist.

Learnings Summarised

Abillity AI and computational vision are going to bring about massive changes in how we manage our farms. Lets make sure we use it to our advantage.
Development Farmers need to be involved from day one of development.
Academics have knowledge and resources for bettering agg

Take home messages

-Be Aware: Network to stay in the know about technology developments.

-Know What You Want: Will the technology deliver tangible benefits.

-Don’t be Leapfrogged:Technological advancements are likely to develop rapidly, be cautious of investing in novel technology with long term ROI.
