What,When,How


In order to make money from adopting technology it has to be a useful technology that makes you better at something you or your customers care about.
You can get a big head start from adopting a technology early on before the majority of your industry follow suit and the price drops to reflect the new production cost. But there are also big advantages to assesing the technology your currently using, is there any low hanging fruit to be had from better utalising the technology you already have. Are you susing it better than your competition.

Be on the lookout for technology that makes you better at something you or your customers care about. Your looking for technology that is likley to become industry norm.
We are at the start of what some are calling the 4th industrial revolution, smart technlogy is apearing that has the ability to out think its human counterpart. Personally I belive computational vision and AI is going to change the agricultural industry to a greater degree than the treactor.
The Ai and computational vision based technologies are going to bring about many useful technologies, just as you are unlikley to outlift a tractor you are not going to out think this new technology at its specific task.
But you will need a keen eye to identify the handfull of usefull technologies from the mass of offerings that are cool/interesting but not useful.
A good point to bear in mind is, will I use it and if there is data can I act on that data.

When: Adopting before the competition

If you want to be competative you need to know your competition.
A good technology choice is one that your competition will be using in the future, the best time to adopt is before it becomes industry norm.
